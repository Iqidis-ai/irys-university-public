# Irys University

The problem we care about is narrow and hard: legal AI has to become usable work product inside real matters. A clause can depend on a definition three documents away. A research answer can depend on a forum limitation. A draft can depend on a partner instruction that never appears in the document itself.

This repository shares how we think about those problems from first principles: what should survive a handoff, what a reviewer must be able to inspect, and what kind of matter record makes later work safer instead of merely faster.

## What This Is

Start here if you care about the gap between an impressive legal AI answer and work a legal team can rely on.

The essays here cover questions like:

- Why do legal AI systems fail when they treat memory as prompt text?
- Why does matter context matter more than a generic chat history?
- Why is auditability an architecture decision, not a feature checkbox?
- What changes when legal work becomes stateful across research, drafting, review, and revision?
- Why does a corrected answer need to change the next draft instead of dying inside one paragraph?
- Where does durable value sit when model fluency becomes common?

The goal is not to publish generic AI commentary. The goal is to explain the mechanisms that determine whether legal AI can be trusted beyond a demo.

## About Irys

Irys is where we build against this thesis: legal AI cannot stop at generating text. It has to preserve the work behind the text, including source support, reviewer correction, matter context, and the state a later task is allowed to rely on.
